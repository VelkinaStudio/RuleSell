# Creative Quality Bar

## Intent
Agents working in this repo should default to standout, portfolio-grade work rather than merely competent UI. The target is strong visual authorship with credible production discipline.

## Output Standard
- Aim for Dribbble-tier craft, but keep flows readable, responsive, and believable in a real product.
- Every major surface should have a clear visual thesis instead of a pile of disconnected nice-looking parts.
- Stronger work usually comes from deciding the atmosphere first: editorial, technical, cinematic, tactile, brutalist, refined industrial, or another deliberate direction.

## Required Design Decisions
Before broad implementation, define:
- visual concept and intended emotional tone
- typography hierarchy and font personality
- semantic palette plus surface and material behavior
- layout rhythm, density, and focal points
- motion language, including reduced-motion behavior
- empty, loading, success, error, and validation states

## Visual System Rules
- Avoid generic AI SaaS tropes such as interchangeable card grids, default blue-purple glows, and safe startup hero patterns.
- Use contrast through scale, spacing, depth, texture, and restraint rather than relying only on bright accents.
- Prefer distinctive typography over default system-safe stacks when the surface allows it.
- Keep dark mode coherent with real depth and surface separation instead of flat charcoal blocks.
- Favor one memorable visual move per route over many weak embellishments.

## 2D and 3D Motion Rules
- Motion must communicate hierarchy, continuity, orientation, or delight with purpose.
- 3D or shader-driven work is best reserved for hero moments, storytelling panels, premium product showcases, or atmospheric backgrounds.
- For app surfaces, motion should stay lighter and support feedback, state change, and trust.
- Any animation-heavy surface must ship with reduced-motion fallbacks and remain usable on mainstream laptop hardware and phones.
- Prefer one strong scene, shader system, or loop over stacking several unrelated animated effects.

## Marketplace-Specific Guidance
- Discovery surfaces should feel dense but calm, with strong metadata hierarchy and trust cues.
- Product detail surfaces should help buyers evaluate compatibility, setup effort, seller credibility, and preview depth quickly.
- Seller, admin, and support surfaces should stay cleaner than marketing pages, but still feel considered and polished.

## Acceptance Check
Before calling a design pass complete, ask:
- Is there a recognizable visual point of view?
- Does the hierarchy read instantly on desktop and mobile?
- Does motion help understanding instead of distracting from it?
- Are degraded states designed, not improvised?
- Would this still feel intentional if screenshots were shared without explanation?
