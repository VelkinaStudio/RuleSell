# Motion and Landing Direction

## Intent
Use motion to communicate hierarchy, continuity, and delight. Do not add motion that obscures usability or slows the application.

## Landing Guidance
- landing can use more ambitious motion, scroll choreography, Spline, video, or 3D elements
- keep a strong narrative flow from hook to trust to differentiation to CTA
- preserve readability and page performance
- use 3D, shaders, or cinematic 2D animation selectively around one strong hero moment instead of many unrelated effects

## App Guidance
- motion should support feedback, not spectacle
- transitions should feel smooth, clear, and low-friction
- reduced-motion fallbacks are required
- app routes should avoid heavy 3D unless it directly improves comprehension or product evaluation

## Quality Bar
- avoid generic canned motion
- prefer a deliberate motion language shared across components and routes
- keep motion responsive and performant on mainstream hardware
- any ambitious animation should degrade cleanly on mobile and reduced-motion devices
