# Design System

## Core Principle
This product should not look like a generic AI SaaS clone. The system should balance a high-end, expressive marketing presence with a restrained, trustworthy product UI.

## Output Standard
- The quality bar is portfolio-grade, Dribbble-tier execution grounded in real product UX.
- Each major surface should have a clear visual thesis rather than relying on generic component assembly.
- Use `creative-quality-bar.md` as the execution guide when the work is visually significant.

## Design Modes
- Marketplace-led entry mode: discovery-first, product-dense, trust-oriented, faster to scan, with selective motion and stronger category/product navigation
- Product mode: calmer layouts, clearer task boundaries, faster scanning, less decorative motion

## Entry Experience Rule
- The default first impression should bias toward marketplace discovery rather than a generic standalone landing page.
- Hero areas should help users search, browse categories, assess trust, and reach products quickly.
- Marketing language should support discovery and conversion, not delay access to inventory.

## Token Areas
- semantic color roles
- typography scale
- spacing scale
- radius scale
- elevation and surface hierarchy
- motion durations and easing

## Structural Expectations
- consistent card, form, list, and filter behavior
- predictable information density
- stronger product metadata hierarchy on listing surfaces
- clear separation between discovery surfaces and transactional surfaces

## Component Expectations
- strong empty, loading, error, success, and validation states
- clear hover, focus, pressed, and disabled states
- trustworthy marketplace cards with strong category, seller, price, and compatibility cues
- fast scanning patterns for search, sort, and filter-heavy pages
